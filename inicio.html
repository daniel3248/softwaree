Comparative: We use comparatives (e.g., "bigger", "faster") to compare two things.

Superlative: We use superlatives (e.g., "biggest", "fastest") to compare three or more things.

Comparatives: These are used to compare two elements. For example:

  • Tall → Taller: "John is taller than Mark."
  • Beautiful → More beautiful: "This painting is more beautiful than that one."
Superlatives: These indicate the highest degree among three or more elements. For example:
  • Tall → Tallest: "John is the tallest in his class."
  • Beautiful → The most beautiful: "This is the most beautiful garden I have ever seen."

Level 2: Comparative and Superlative Adjectives

In this level, we will test your knowledge of comparative and superlative adjectives in English. Remember that comparatives are used to compare two things, while superlatives are used to express the highest degree among three or more items.

Comparatives

Comparatives are formed using "more" for adjectives with two or more syllables. For example:

  • difficult → more difficult
  • Sentence: This test is more difficult than the last one.

Superlatives

Superlatives are formed using "most" for adjectives with two or more syllables. For example:

  • beautiful → most beautiful
  • Sentence: This is the most beautiful place I have ever seen.
